A voice AI for India's helplines that ensures being understood comes before being answered.

Voca is the AI bridge between citizen and agent — built for Theme 12 of the AI for Bharat 2 hackathon. It hears the caller in her dialect, restates back what it understood, only then acts. When confidence drops or distress spikes, it hands off to a human agent — with full context, sentiment timeline, and a suggested opening line.

The thesis

Verify before act. The four-state lifecycle is the load-bearing rule of the system.

Most voice AI products optimize for hear → respond. Voca inverts this: hear → restate → verify → only then act. The verification turn is not a usability afterthought. It is the structural element on which the whole product rests.

The one rule we will not break: no interpretation, however confident, is acted upon without explicit citizen confirmation — except when distress and risk_signal both exceed threshold, in which case Voca rapid-acks and hands off to a human in parallel.
How it works

Three engineered layers for dialect — not a bigger model and a hope.

Generic LLMs handle written Hindi and written Kannada. They struggle with spoken Mandya Kannada, code-mixed Hinglish, and cultural shorthand that depends on local context. Voca addresses each layer deliberately.

Layer 1

Acoustic

AI4Bharat IndicConformer for streaming STT across 22 Indian languages. Trained on dialect-tagged data — Mandya, Belagavi, Mangaluru Kannada, Bhojpuri, Telangana Telugu. n-best hypotheses preserved; ambiguity is deferred to context, not collapsed early.

Layer 2

Lexical

Sarvam-1 fine-tuned on a curated grievance corpus and Karnataka context. Indic-native tokenizer is 3–4× more efficient than GPT-style on Devanagari and Kannada — direct latency and cost win on every turn.

Layer 3

Cultural

Honorific normalizer (neevu / neenu), curated idiom dictionary, district-entity gazetteer (pgvector). When the citizen says "galli", we retrieve their stated location. Deterministic — code, not stochastic.

Sentiment

Sentiment is a routing signal, not a UI badge.

Most voice products treat emotion as a colored dot on a dashboard. For 1092, it must change the system's behavior. Voca detects six states from acoustic and lexical signals, continuously, at 500ms granularity.

SignalDetectionSystem behavior
Distress + urgency high Prosody (pitch jitter, energy spike) + lexical risk-words Bypass full verification · rapid-ack · parallel handover
Sustained confusion Continuous low-confidence STT + slow turn-taking Switch to simpler register · slower TTS pace · shorter sentences
Anger at the system Lexical anger toward AI + escalating volume Immediate handover · no placation loop · full context to agent
Fear escalating Prosodic fear signature growing across turns Soft handover trigger · agent prepared · empathetic acknowledgement
Neutral · calm Default state Standard verification flow
Architecture

One stack. On-prem-capable. No PII to hosted LLMs.

Voca's production path is self-hosted Indian foundation models — Sarvam-1 for interpretation, AI4Bharat IndicConformer for STT, IndicTTS for voice. Bhashini is wired in as a government-blessed fallback. Hosted LLMs are used only at development time on synthetic data.

Why now

Three step-changes that make this possible in 2026, and not before.

01

Indic foundation models matured

AI4Bharat (IIT Madras) and Sarvam AI shipped open, dialect-aware models that didn't exist 18 months ago. The acoustic gap on rural Kannada and Hindi closed from "frustrating" to "usable."

02

Real-time voice infrastructure commoditized

LiveKit Agents, Pipecat, sub-100ms streaming TTS — research-grade in 2024, plumbing in 2026. A 2-person team can plug them together in two weeks.

03

Government readiness

Bhashini is operational and open for integration. GeM is ready to procure AI services that respect Indian sovereign-cloud requirements. Five years ago this would have died at the procurement gate.

Roadmap

Karnataka first. Then every helpline in Bharat — on the same backbone.

Voca's pipeline is language-agnostic at the orchestration layer. Adding a new Indian language is a three-step process — confirm acoustic and TTS coverage, curate the cultural KB for that region, collect a small fine-tune set. Estimated ~2 weeks per language to production quality.
